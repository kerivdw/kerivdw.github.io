Learning Plan Bootcamp

10 February 2023

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

On completion of this course, my goal is to apply for software development roles. With the view to gaining a role in a company where I am able to continue to build on the skills I have learnt at dev academy. I would like to work in a company that is small enough that I have enough variety and opportunity to learn new things in my work, but large enough that there are experienced people that I am able to learn from to grow my own skill set.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

When it comes to learning, I am quite focused and disciplined. I am independent and resilient to challenges. I enjoy learning new things, which makes the process enjoyable for me.

In terms of limitations, I can become overwhelmed when there is a lot of content. I need to pause a little and make sure I have understood everything rather than continuing through it.

I think also my shyness in groups and situations where it is required to talk about myself personally could be challenging initially but hopefully it will get more comfortable with practice.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I would say that on the whole, my time management is fairly good. When there are things that are particularly challenging I do lose track of time. I will need to be strict on myself to timebox those challenges, ask for help and take breaks. So that I don’t let the other team members down in shared challenges.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Solid collaboration skills, tech is built in teams. This means working with others to make all the parts come together. This is something I am excited to practice.

With best intentions, when working in teams from time to time conflict will still arise. This is something I am not fully confident with. I would love to have stronger skills in dealing with conflict.

Being able to process other people's feelings and negotiate these situations well, would be a great skill to have. Having good understanding and strategies for handling these types of situations would be a success for me.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

My understanding is that in the bootcamp is the practical part of the flipped learning concept. We have spent the last 5 weeks being supported through a self learning phase, this part will be a phase where we will put that knowledge into practice in groups in real world examples with real world feedback.

My expectation is that the bootcamp team is there to facilitate that process;

  • by facilitating group learning, using the strength of the group to solve problems
  • by providing coaching and technical support on how to approach different problems
  • by providing coaching and support in human skills on how to deal with different situations.
  • by providing feedback on progress at intervals

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

My expectation of myself during bootcamp is that I will have an opportunity to practice and build on the technical and human skills learnt in foundation. Working with others in a respectful, predictable and transparent way.

I will manage my workload through planning my work well. Using my calendar to keep a track of lectures and catch up’s that I have planned. Blocking out focused time in my calendar to complete any tasks I have agreed to.

I will also ensure that during this time I will take responsibility for my own well being. Ensuring that I am eating well, getting enough rest and exercising during this period.

I will help myself when I am blocked by setting timeboxes for a problem, if I am stuck I can look at previous lessons or search google for the answer. If this does not unblock me I can ask someone in my team on discord for help. If I am still not able to find the answer I can ask one of the course facilitators to help me out. If I have any situations that occur outside of the course I can let one of the course facilitators know in a timely way.
